What I Do
  • Design art based on requests submitted by the GM project manager

What I Do NOT Do
  • Design without a complete, approved request — all content must be provided upfront
  • Determine warning language — a toxicology report or explicit written instructions are required
  • Make decisions on content — addresses, license numbers, phone numbers, and codes are the requestor's responsibility
  • Determine compliance sizing or placement — the requestor must define this
  • Review your request before working on it — what you submit is what gets designed
!
Required Before I Can Begin
  • Dieline File Must show all boundaries including bleed and cut lines, clearly labeled. Must be editable in Adobe or PDF format. This file comes from your printer and depends on the supplier and/or container being filled.
  • All Label Content Addresses, license numbers, phone numbers, internal codes, and all other text — final and confirmed. If it is wrong, the artwork will be wrong.
  • Marketing Statements Provide in English plus any additional languages required on the label.
  • Product Names — 3 Languages Required Refer to the Translations Part Noun Name Master File. You must obtain all 3 product names from this list. If the name is not listed, add it — Marketing/Product Management are co-owners and can assist.
  • International Label Report Must be attached to your request. Found in the Chemical Label Process on the main page.
  • Toxicology Report Required for warning language — or provide explicit written instructions specifying exactly what the warnings must say.
  • Compliance Requirements Label size, required language placement, and all regulatory specs — defined and confirmed before submitting.
